## Executive Summary

AmPac Business Capital operates as a small business lender focused on SBA 504 loans and community loans across the Southwest United States. As an SBA-participating lender serving underserved markets, AMPAC is subject to multiple overlapping federal regulatory frameworks administered by the Small Business Administration, U.S. Department of Treasury, Federal Reserve, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), and Department of Justice.

This AMP identifies all applicable compliance frameworks and recommends priority implementation areas.

## Applicable Compliance Frameworks

### 1. **SBA LOAN PROGRAMS & REGULATION**

#### Governing Authority
- **SBA Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) 50 10** - Lender and Development Company Loan Programs
- **13 CFR Part 120** - Small Business Administration Loan Programs
- **SBA Form 1919** - Statement of Personal History & Conflict of Interest

#### Primary Compliance Requirements

**Borrower Ownership Verification** *(EFFECTIVE MARCH 1, 2026)*
- 100% of direct and indirect owners of borrower must be U.S. citizens or U.S. nationals
- All owners must maintain principal residence in U.S., territories, or possessions
- Legal permanent residents (green card holders) **cannot** hold any ownership interest
- Lenders responsible for verifying ownership through documentation and tracing indirect interests through multiple entity layers
- *Impact:* Expanded due diligence, longer underwriting timelines, higher compliance costs

**SBA 504 Loan Program Specifics**
- Serve as active participant in CDC (Certified Development Company) ecosystem
- Perform first mortgage underwriting and origination
- Conduct credit analysis ensuring reasonable assurance of repayment from business cash flow
- Comply with SBA lending limits and eligibility requirements
- Maintain minimum lending activity levels

**Loan Amount & Eligibility Changes** *(EFFECTIVE MARCH 1, 2026)*
- Maximum 7(a) Small Loan amount reduced from $500,000 to $350,000
- Upfront guaranty fees and lender service fees reinstated (pandemic-era zero-fee period ended)
- Loan term and rate adjustments per current SBA fee schedule
- Borrower eligibility verification responsibility shifted to lenders

**SBSS Score Transition** *(EFFECTIVE MARCH 1, 2026)*
- FICO SBSS score sunset; lenders may use alternative credit scoring tools
- Underwriting models may need adjustment

**Credit Underwriting Standards**
- Must document specific reasons credit is unavailable elsewhere with supporting documentation
- Cash flow analysis from business operations primary repayment source
- Collateral secondary to cash flow repayment capacity
- Deny applications lacking reasonable assurance of repayment despite available collateral

#### Compliance Infrastructure Required
- Ownership verification documentation procedures and training
- Updated underwriting guidelines reflecting March 1, 2026 changes
- Revised credit scoring/underwriting models
- SBA regulation monitoring and policy update procedures
- Form 1919 collection and retention processes

### 2. **COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT FINANCIAL INSTITUTION (CDFI) COMPLIANCE**

#### Governing Authority
- **12 CFR Part 1805** - Community Development Financial Institutions Program
- **U.S. Department of Treasury CDFI Fund**
- **CDFI Certification Application & Requirements**

#### Primary Compliance Requirements

**Target Market & Investment Area Service**
- Serve one or more Investment Areas and/or Targeted Populations as defined by CDFI Fund
- Demonstrate consistent Financial Products/Services delivery to target markets
- Maintain track record of providing Development Services alongside financing products
- Document geographic and demographic focus areas

**Anti-Discrimination & Civil Rights Compliance**
- Cannot violate federal anti-discrimination laws
- **No** race, ethnicity, or sex-based employment or financial preferences/set-asides (unless federally authorized)
- Adopt, implement, and maintain anti-discrimination policies and procedures
- Certify annually to CDFI Fund regarding policy existence and administration
- Make compliance documentation available for CDFI Fund review

**Federal Oversight & Monitoring**
- Provide full access to books, documents, records, financial statements to U.S. Government representatives
- Complete annual financial reporting to Treasury CDFI Fund
- Participate in CDFI Fund impact assessments and data collection
- Maintain compliance with all record retention requirements

**CDFI Certification Maintenance**
- Maintain certified status with U.S. Treasury
- Comply with all certification conditions and requirements
- Report any material changes in business operations or structure
- Support periodic CDFI Fund audits and examinations

#### Compliance Infrastructure Required
- Target market and investment area documentation
- Development Services program design and tracking
- Anti-discrimination policy adoption and annual certification
- Annual CDFI Fund reporting and data submission
- Records management system for Treasury oversight access
- Governance structure supporting CDFI mission

### 3. **FAIR LENDING & EQUAL CREDIT OPPORTUNITY (ECOA)**

#### Governing Authority
- **Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)** & **Regulation B (12 CFR Part 202)**
- **Fair Housing Act (FHA)**
- **Enforced by:** CFPB, FDIC, Federal Reserve, NCUA, DOJ

#### Primary Compliance Requirements

**Prohibited Discrimination Basis**
Cannot discriminate based on:
- Race, color, religion, national origin
- Sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, gender nonconformity with sex-based stereotypes
- Marital status
- Age (for applicants with capacity to contract)
- Receipt of public assistance income
- Exercise of consumer credit protection law rights

**Prohibited Lending Practices**
- Cannot refuse to provide credit information or services
- Cannot discourage applications through selective encouragement
- Cannot refuse credit or use different credit extension standards
- Cannot vary credit terms based on prohibited basis
- Cannot selectively market different products to protected classes

**Disparate Impact Liability**
- Even facially neutral policies creating disparate impact by race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, or age constitute violation
- Lenders must monitor underwriting, pricing, and marketing for disparate impact

**Regulation B Compliance Components**
- Adverse action notices (written explanation of credit denial)
- Credit terms disclosure and documentation
- Prohibited information requests (race, religion, national origin on app forms - *some permissible for monitoring only*)
- Credit scoring system impact assessment
- Loan file documentation standards

#### Compliance Infrastructure Required
- Fair lending compliance officer designation
- Written fair lending policies and procedures
- Regular training for lending staff and decision-makers
- Fair lending monitoring and testing programs (underwriting, pricing, marketing, origination)
- Adverse action notice procedures
- Loan file documentation audit procedures
- Compliance with CFPB guidance on credit discrimination

### 5. **ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) & KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC)**

#### Governing Authority
- **Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)** - 31 U.S.C. § 5301 et seq.
- **Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)** - 31 CFR § 1020
- **Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)** - 50 U.S.C. § 4401 et seq.
- **Enforced by:** FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network), OCC, Federal Reserve, FDIC

#### Primary Compliance Requirements

**Know Your Customer (KYC)**
- Verify identity of borrowers at origination
- Obtain identifying information: name, address, date of birth, tax ID
- Verify identity through documentary evidence (ID, tax returns, bank statements)
- Maintain customer identification records

**Customer Due Diligence (CDD)**
- Understand nature and purpose of customer relationships
- Conduct ongoing monitoring of customer activity
- Identify beneficial owners of business borrowers
- Assess money laundering risk based on customer profile, geography, products

**Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)**
- May be required for higher-risk customers (shell companies, high-risk jurisdictions, politically exposed persons)
- Document rationale for EDD application

**OFAC Screening**
- Screen all borrowers and beneficial owners against OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list
- Screen against other government watch lists (FBI, NSA, etc.)
- Document screening procedures and results
- No transactions with SDNs or blocked entities

**Suspicious Activity Reporting (SARs)**
- Monitor for suspicious borrower behavior (structuring, unusual transaction patterns)
- File SARs when transactions totaling $5,000+ involve known or suspected money laundering or terrorist financing
- SARs must be filed within 30 days of detecting suspicious activity
- Document SAR decisions and maintain audit trail
- Criminal disclosure rules apply (FinCEN consent before disclosure)

**BSA Compliance Program**
- Designate BSA Compliance Officer
- Develop written AML/BSA policies and procedures
- Conduct annual independent audit of AML/BSA program
- Train employees on AML/BSA requirements
- Document and report all compliance activities

#### Compliance Infrastructure Required
- KYC documentation procedures (application, ID verification, beneficial owner identification)
- OFAC screening automation and procedures
- CDD risk assessment framework
- SARs filing procedures and tracking system
- AML/BSA compliance officer designation
- Employee training program on AML/KYC
- Annual audit and testing
- Independent testing and monitoring

### 6. **FAIR CREDIT REPORTING ACT (FCRA) COMPLIANCE**

#### Governing Authority
- **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)** - 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.
- **Enforced by:** CFPB, FTC, FDIC, Federal Reserve

#### Primary Compliance Requirements

**Credit Reporting Use Disclosures**
- Obtain consumer consent before requesting credit reports
- Provide clear notice that credit reports will be obtained
- Disclose intended use of credit information

**Adverse Action Notices**
- When credit decision is based wholly or partly on credit report information, provide adverse action notice
- Include: specific reasons for adverse action, name/address/phone of credit reporting agency
- Provide consumer's right to dispute and obtain free credit report

**Accuracy & Dispute Resolution**
- Maintain accurate records of applicant information
- Investigate consumer disputes regarding accuracy of information reported
- Correct inaccurate information in company records

**Specialty Consumer Reports**
- If obtaining credit reports beyond traditional tri-merge credit bureau reports, may have special FCRA requirements
- Alternative data sources (payment history, alternative credit data) require specific disclosures

#### Compliance Infrastructure Required
- Disclosure and consent procedures for credit report ordering
- Adverse action notice system
- Record retention for FCRA documentation
- Dispute handling procedures
- Credit report provider selection and management
- FCRA training for underwriting and decision-making staff

### 7. **DATA PRIVACY & CONSUMER INFORMATION SECURITY**

#### Governing Authority
- **Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)** - Standards for Safeguarding Customer Information (15 USC 6801-6809)
- **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)** & **California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)** *(due to CA operations)*
- **California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA)**
- **Safeguards Rule** - 16 CFR Part 314 (FTC/CFPB)
- **Privacy Rule** - 16 CFR Part 313 (FTC/CFPB)
- **Enforced by:** FTC, CFPB, State AG, California Privacy Protection Agency

#### Primary Compliance Requirements

**Safeguards Rule - Information Security Program**
- Appoint qualified Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Develop, implement, maintain written information security program
- Conduct annual risk assessments of customer information security
- Implement administrative, technical, physical safeguards:
  - Access controls and employee training
  - Data encryption for data in transit and at rest
  - Incident response and breach notification procedures
  - Vendor management and third-party oversight
  - Audit and testing of security controls
- Monitor and test security measures continuously

**Privacy Rule - Privacy Notice & Policy**
- Provide clear privacy notice to borrowers explaining:
  - What information is collected
  - How information is used
  - Categories of parties with whom information may be shared
  - Customer opt-out rights for information sharing
- Obtain opt-out consent for non-affiliated third-party sharing (where applicable)
- Maintain privacy policy for internal compliance

**California Privacy Laws (CCPA/CPRA)**
- Apply to for-profit entities collecting California residents' personal information
- Provide California Consumer Privacy Notices (right to know, delete, opt-out, non-discrimination)
- Establish processes for consumer rights requests (access, deletion, correction, portability)
- Implement privacy by design
- Disclose third parties with whom data is shared

**Incident Response & Breach Notification**
- Develop incident response plan for security breaches
- Notify affected consumers without unreasonable delay if breach involves unencrypted personal information
- Notify California Attorney General if breach affects 500+ California residents
- Document breach response and regulatory notifications

#### Compliance Infrastructure Required
- Data classification and inventory
- Risk assessment documentation
- Information security program and policies
- CISO designation and responsibilities
- Encryption standards and implementation (data in transit, at rest)
- Access control procedures
- Employee training on information handling
- Third-party vendor management agreements
- Incident response plan and procedures
- Breach notification procedures
- Privacy notice and opt-out procedures
- California consumer rights request handling

### 8. **BORROWER PRIVACY & FEDERAL CONSUMER PROTECTION REGULATIONS**

#### Governing Authority
- **Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act** - 15 U.S.C. § 1601 et seq.
- **Truth in Lending Act (TILA)** - 12 CFR Part 226 (Regulation Z)
- **Truth in Savings Act (TISA)** - 12 CFR Part 230
- **Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)** - Previously covered; intersects here for loan terms
- **Enforced by:** CFPB, Federal Reserve, FDIC, OCC

#### Primary Compliance Requirements

**Truth in Lending (TILA) - Loan Disclosure**
*(Applies if AMPAC directly extends credit to borrowers)*
- Provide Loan Estimate within 3 business days of application
- Include: loan amount, interest rate, finance charges, payment amounts, loan terms
- Provide Closing Disclosure at least 3 business days before closing
- Clear, conspicuous disclosure of APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
- If applicant's credit score is material to decision, disclose score and score range
- Rescission rights for certain closed-end credit transactions

**Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (UDAAP)**
- Cannot engage in unfair, deceptive, or abusive lending practices
- Unfair: causes substantial injury to consumers that is not reasonably avoidable and is not outweighed by countervailing benefits
- Deceptive: material misrepresentation or omission likely to mislead reasonable consumer
- Abusive: takes unreasonable advantage of consumer vulnerability or lack of understanding
- Examples: Hidden fees, misleading APR advertising, aggressive collection practices

**Mortgage Disclosure Requirements** *(If originating real estate-secured loans)*
- High-cost mortgage disclosures (loans above rate/fee thresholds)
- APR and fee disclosures
- Restrictions on certain loan features and prepayment penalties

#### Compliance Infrastructure Required
- Loan disclosure procedures and forms
- Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure templates
- Credit score disclosure procedures (when used in decision-making)
- UDAAP monitoring and training
- Advertising compliance review
- Loan file documentation standards
